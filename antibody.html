Antibody Test.

General Information

  • The Covid-19 antibody test is NOT currently recommended for use in diagnosing Covid or testing for Covid immunity
  • Only tests for the presence of antibodies to viral antigens like the nucleocapsid (N) and spike (S) protein
  • Antibody presence can be detected within days (up to weeks) following Covid infection/vaccination
  • Thus it cannot determine the presence or absence of Covid at time of test
  • A positive result means the testee has Covid (and rarely other coronaviruses) antibodies from either a previous infection or vaccination. This is due to the vaccines construction to encode the S protein
  • Antibody tests tend to have higher false positive rates, as other coronaviruses can encode the proteins that the test looks for

Types of Antibody Testing

Point-of-care (POC)

  • Provides results within minutes using lateral flow immunoassay devices to detect antibodies in serums, plasma, or blood using a blood prick

Neutralizing Antibody Detection

  • Use a test tube or culture dish to check for antibodies to Covid-19
  • 3 categories of neutralization tests:
    • Virus neutralization tests: uses reporter proteins for Covid and the results can take up to 5 days
    • Pseudovirus neutralization tests: involves a recombinant pseudovirus (a virus that incorporates the S protein of Covid-19) to test for antibody presence
    • Surrogate neutralization tests: does not require a culture, and also forgoes the need for using native viral particles. Can easily be adapted to the ELISA antibody detection format

Interpretation of Results

The following table shows the current interpretation of antibody test results.

Anti-S Antibody Anti-N Antibody Interpretation
Present Present Previously infected, cannot infer if vaccinated
Present Not present Not previously infected, can infer if vaccinated
Not present Not present Not previously infected or vaccinated
